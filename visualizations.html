My day job deals with cancer genomics. To promote better data access and comprehension. I designed a quite a few proof-of-concept visualizations for my team at work to explore better ways of exploring and interacting with genomics data. As with most proof of concepts, many of these are quite rough around the edges, they are merely meant to illustrate an idea.


Using Arc-Diagram for set interactions

Designed to handle set-analyses and to be used in place of the more venerable Venn/Euler diagrams. The same subsets are highlighted across multiple encompassing sets, with the arcs acting as guides. The key advantages are:

  • Arc-digram scales better, we can comfortably fit 5-6 set intersections before usability becomes an issue.
  • We don't have degenerate cases where the overlapping propotions cannot be accurated represented (with circles).

Using small multiples to investigate donor traits

A donor has many physical and clinical traits; gender, status, age...etc. In order to see how one trait reacts with another, I designed a small multiple bar charts to do this.

The traits are listed as dimensions, the point is to see how dimension #2 varies across unique values of dimension #1. For example one may want to see how vital status varies arcoss different primary cancer sites.

Enhanced bar chart

Bar charts are ubiquitous. But can we do a bit more?
My take has the following twists:

  • A guide line showing the position on the axis when interacting with the chart, this allows one to easily compare the bars value against others.
  • Pagination. Yep, this isn't as nice as focus-context technique, however this is more compact which was a goal at the time.
